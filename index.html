Quick Start

Follow these steps in the field or while reviewing recordings.

  1. 1
    Choose the target animal
    On the home screen, pick Elk, Deer, or Turkey. The app automatically loads the correct acoustic model and score weights for that species.
  2. 2
    Let the phone “listen”
    Hold the iPhone with the microphone pointed toward the sound source. The app continuously fills a 5-second buffer with audio from the built-in mic while you wait for a call.
  3. 3
    Tap Analyze
    After the call finishes, tap the Analyze button. The app runs a set of bio-acoustic checks (pitch stability, harmonics, band energy, noise, envelope shape, etc.) and computes a 0–1 score for “likely real”.
  4. 4
    Read the score & spectrogram
    The score widget changes color from red (very synthetic) to green (very natural), and the spectrogram shows the frequency structure of the call over time.
  5. 5
    Adjust sensitivity (optional)
    In Settings, you can tweak how strict the app is. If you want to flag more “maybe real” calls, reduce strictness. If you only want very clean, natural calls to score high, increase strictness.

How the score works

The app does not guarantee that a call is real or fake. Instead, it analyzes the sound and estimates how closely it matches typical bio-acoustic patterns for natural calls.

Score 0.00 – 1.00
  • 0.0 – 0.3: very synthetic / unlikely natural
  • 0.3 – 0.7: mixed evidence / uncertain
  • 0.7 – 1.0: strongly natural-like

Always combine the app’s score with your own judgment, regulations, and local conditions. This is a decision-support tool, not a rule-making device.

Field Tips

  • Keep the iPhone mic unobstructed (no case over the mic ports).
  • Avoid rubbing noises, wind directly on the mic, or loud talking.
  • Use headphones if you want to replay recorded calls around others.
  • Note weather conditions (wind, rain, heavy fog can affect acoustics).
